05 — Research Verdict

🏆LightGBM wins classification

LightGBM achieves 77.43% accuracy vs GA-NN's 62.08% — a +15% margin — while training 2× faster. Gradient boosting decisively handles tabular, imbalanced material datasets better than an evolutionary-tuned MLP.

📐Boosting dominates regression

For lattice parameter prediction, LightGBM reaches R²=52.4% vs GA-NN's 2.4% on parameter a. The MLP struggles with the non-linear atomic-level correlations — exactly the limitation the original paper identified.

⚡Speed vs complexity

GA-NN training involves evolutionary overhead (200 generations × 50 population). Boosting models train in under 300ms with no GA loop. For production deployment in materials discovery, this matters enormously.

🔬Key research insight

The Tolerance Factor (26.8%) and Valence A (26.0%) dominate feature importance in XGBoost — consistent with Goldschmidt's theory. GA-NN cannot extract this physics-aligned hierarchy as efficiently.
